1. SHEATH BLIGTH:

Sheath blight, caused by the fungus Rhizoctonia solani, is a significant disease that affects rice crops, though it can also impact other plants. The pathogen primarily targets the lower part of the rice plant, particularly the leaf sheath, but it can also affect stems and roots

  • -> Symptoms: Irregular, water-soaked lesions on the leaf sheath, turning brown or grayish. The affected parts can dry out, leading to yield loss.
  • -> Crops Affected: Rice.

SOLVING TECHNIQUES OF SHEATH BLIGTH DISEASE:
  • 1. Use resistant rice varieties.
  • 2. Apply fungicides like azoxystrobin or propiconazole.
  • 3. Maintain proper spacing for good air circulation.
  • 4. Drain water before applying fungicides to ensure proper contact.

2. DROUGHT STRESS

Drought stress in rice (Oryza sativa) is a critical issue, especially in regions that rely on rain-fed rice cultivation or face water scarcity due to climate change. Rice is a water-intensive crop, and its growth and development are highly sensitive to water availability.

  • ->Symptoms: Wilting, yellowing, and drying of leaves, poor root development, and stunted growth.
  • ->Crops Affected: Rice.

SOLVING TECHNIQUES OF DROUGHT STRESS DISEASE:
  • 1. Use drought-resistant rice varieties.
  • 2. Apply efficient irrigation systems like drip or sprinkler irrigation.
  • 3. Use mulch to conserve soil moisture.
  • 4. Practice rainwater harvesting to supplement irrigation.

3. FOOT ROT

The rice stem borer (Scirpophaga excerptalis and Scirpophaga excerptalis), commonly known as white rice stem borer, is one of the most destructive insect pests affecting rice crops worldwide. It causes significant damage by boring into the stems of rice plants, leading to yield loss, reduced quality, and sometimes complete crop failure.

  • Symptoms: White or brown streaks on stems, leading to lodging and stunted growth.
  • Crops Affected: Rice.

SOLVING TECHNIQUES OF FOOT ROT DISEASE:
  • 1. Use resistant rice varieties.
  • 2. Apply insecticides like chlorpyrifos to control stem borers.
  • 3. Practice crop rotation and sanitation to reduce pest pressure.

4. FALSE SMUT

False smut (Ustilaginoidea virens) is a fungal disease that affects rice (Oryza sativa) and can lead to significant yield losses and poor grain quality. It primarily impacts the rice panicle (the flowering and fruiting structure), causing abnormal development of the grains and reducing the quality of the rice produced.

  • ->Symptoms: Small, greenish, or orange-colored balls of infected tissue that appear on panicles, replacing healthy grains.
  • ->Crops Affected: Rice.

SOLVING TECHNIQUES OF FALSE SMUT DISEASE:
  • 1. Use resistant rice varieties.
  • 2. Apply fungicides like propiconazole or tebuconazole.
  • 3. Remove infected plants and practice field sanitation.

5. CYLINDROCALDIUM BLACK ROT

Cylindrocladium Black Rot (CBR) in peanuts is a destructive disease caused by the fungus Cylindrocladium parasiticum. It is a significant soil-borne disease that primarily affects peanut plants (Arachis hypogaea) in warm and humid conditions. CBR can cause severe damage to peanut crops, leading to yield loss and poor quality, especially during critical stages of plant development.

  • ->Symptoms: Initial symptoms include wilting of plants, followed by dark streaks or lesions on stems. The infected plants can collapse, and the roots may show black rot.
  • ->Crops Affected: Peanuts.

SOLVING TECHNIQUES OF CYLINDROCALDIUM BLACK ROT DISEASE:
  • 1. Use resistant varieties.
  • 2. Apply fungicides such as metalaxyl or flutolanil.
  • 3. Avoid excessive irrigation to reduce soil moisture around the roots.

6. PEANUT POD BORER

The Peanut Pod Borer (Earis vitella), also known as the peanut butterfly or peanut podworm, is a significant pest of peanuts (Arachis hypogaea), particularly in regions with warm climates. The larvae of this pest feed on peanut pods, leading to damage that can reduce both the yield and quality of the crop.

  • ->Symptoms: Bores into the pods, causing rotting and deformities in the peanut kernels.
  • ->Crops Affected: Peanuts.

SOLVING TECHNIQUES OF PEANUT POD BORER DISEASE:
  • 1. Apply insecticides to control pod borers.
  • 2. Practice crop rotation to break the pest's lifecycle.
  • 3. Remove infected plant debris.

7. TOBACOO BUDWORM

The Tobacco Budworm (Helicoverpa virescens) is a significant pest of many crops, including peanuts (Arachis hypogaea), and is known for causing substantial damage to the plant, particularly to the flowers, pods, and seeds. This pest is closely related to other agricultural pests like the Cotton Bollworm (Helicoverpa zea), and its larvae are the damaging life stage.

  • ->Symptoms: Damaged pods and flowers, feeding damage on the flowers and developing pods.
  • ->Crops Affected: Peanuts.

SOLVING TECHNIQUES OF TOBACOO BUDWORM DISEASE:
  • 1. Apply insecticides such as pyrethroids or Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt).
  • 2. Practice crop rotation to disrupt pest life cycles.

8. RED ROT

Red Rot in sugarcane (Sorghum bicolor) is a serious fungal disease caused by Colletotrichum falcatum, a pathogen that severely impacts the growth, yield, and quality of sugarcane crops. The disease is named for the red, often darkened lesions it causes in the affected tissues of the plant.

  • ->Symptoms: Red or dark streaks in the vascular tissue of the cane, leading to a breakdown of the stem and a rotting odor. The disease causes severe yield loss and stunting.
  • ->Crops Affected: Sugarcane.

SOLVING TECHNIQUES OF RED ROT DISEASE:
  • 1. Use resistant sugarcane varieties.
  • 2. Remove and destroy infected stalks and plant debris.
  • 3. Apply fungicides like propiconazole or copper-based fungicides.
  • 4. Maintain proper field drainage.

9. FUSARIUM WILT

Fusarium Wilt in sugarcane (Saccharum officinarum) is a destructive disease caused by the fungus Fusarium oxysporum f.sp. sacchari. This pathogen is responsible for significant yield losses and can spread rapidly within a sugarcane field if not properly managed. Fusarium wilt primarily affects the vascular system of the plant, leading to wilting, stunting, and eventual death of the sugarcane plant.

  • ->Symptoms: Yellowing of older leaves, wilting, and premature plant death. The vascular tissues in the stem become discolored.
  • ->Crops Affected: Sugarcane.

SOLVING TECHNIQUES OF FUSARIUM WILT DISEASE:
  • 1. Practice crop rotation with non-host plants.
  • 2. Improve soil drainage and aeration.
  • 3. Remove and destroy infected plants.

10. SESAME WILT

Sesame Wilt is a serious disease in sesame (Sesamum indicum) caused by a variety of pathogens, with the most common being Fusarium oxysporum f.sp. sesami, a fungal pathogen. Other fungal pathogens, such as Verticillium dahliae and Rhizoctonia solani, can also cause similar wilt symptoms.

  • ->Symptoms: Yellowing, wilting, and leaf drop, usually starting on one side of the plant. The vascular tissue may show a dark discoloration.
  • ->Crops Affected: Sesame.

SOLVING TECHNIQUES OF SESAME WILT DISEASE:
  • 1. Use resistant sesame varieties if available.
  • 2. Practice crop rotation with non-host crops to break the disease cycle.
  • 3. Avoid excessive irrigation and improve drainage to reduce stress.

11. FUNGAL DISEASE:

Fungi are one of the most common pathogens that affect crops. They can cause root rot, leaf blights, and wilting. Some common fungal diseases include:

  • -> Rusts: Affect leaves, stems, and other plant parts. Example: Wheat rust.
  • -> Blights: Lead to rapid tissue decay. Example: Late blight in potatoes and tomatoes.
  • -> Molds: Can lead to mildew or gray mold. Example: Downy mildew on grapes.

SOLVING TECHNIQUES OF FUNGAL DISEASE:
  • 1. Remove Infected Plant Parts
  • 2. Improve Air Circulation
  • 3. Watering Practices
  • 4. Proper Soil Drainage

12. BARLEY DISEASE:

Bacteria can infect plants and cause wilting, discoloration, or the death of the plant. Some bacterial diseases include:

  • -> Bacterial Wilt: Affects crops like tomatoes and potatoes.
  • -> Bacterial Blight: Common in rice and beans.
  • -> Crown Gall: Caused by Agrobacterium tumefaciens, infecting many crops like fruit trees.

SOLVING TECHNIQUES OF BACTERIAL DISEASE:
  • 1. Remove infected parts of the plant and sanitize tools.
  • 2. Apply bactericides (such as copper-based products) or use biological control agents.
  • 3. Improve watering and air circulation to reduce moisture on plant surfaces.
  • 4. Choose resistant varieties and practice crop rotation.
